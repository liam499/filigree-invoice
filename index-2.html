The Problem

Invoice fraud — a $2.9B annual problem with no clean defence

Business email compromise (BEC) and invoice tampering account for the largest single category of reported business cybercrime loss in the US. The standard attack is trivial: intercept a legitimate vendor invoice, alter the payment instructions — IBAN, account number, amount — and forward. The payer pays the attacker. The vendor chases an unpaid invoice. Nobody notices for weeks.

Signatures sit beside the invoice, not within it
DKIM, S/MIME, PDF signature blocks — all attached separately. Strip the attachment, re-render the PDF, paste the text into a new email, and the document continues to circulate without its proof.
Verification UX kills adoption
Existing schemes require certificate management, plug-ins, or specialist tooling. AP clerks don't have time, and the protections that exist go unused.
The Solution

Make the proof and the invoice inseparable

Filigree weaves an ECDSA signature into the invoice text itself, distributed across every character via invisible Unicode. The authentication layer cannot be stripped because it is the invoice. Change a single digit of the IBAN — verification fails. Adjust the amount — verification fails. The payer pastes the invoice into the verifier, pastes the vendor's public key, and gets a binary answer in under a second. No certificate authority, no install, no plug-in.

Properties

Properties that matter for AP integration

Intrinsic
The signature lives inside the invoice text, distributed across every character. Cannot be stripped without destroying the visible content.
Verifier-friendly
Paste invoice, paste public key, click verify. Under thirty seconds. No install, no certificate management, no specialist knowledge required.
Definitive
Verification is binary. Pass or fail. No partial results, no "warning" states for a clerk to interpret wrongly under pressure.
Transparent to existing systems
Signed invoices render identically to unsigned ones in every email client, PDF reader, and ERP. Systems without Filigree are unaffected.
Standard cryptography
ECDSA P-256 and SHA-256. The innovation is architectural — where the signature lives — not in the primitives.
Offline-verifiable
No CA, no OCSP, no blockchain. The invoice and the vendor's public key are all the AP team needs.
