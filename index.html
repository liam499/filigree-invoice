The problem

Invoice fraud has no clean defence.

Business email compromise — intercept a vendor's invoice, alter the payment instructions, forward — runs in the billions of dollars of reported loss annually (FBI IC3, $2.9B in 2023; consistently among the largest categories of reported business cybercrime loss). Existing protections sit beside the document, not within it: signature blocks can be stripped, certificate chains break on copy-paste, and verification typically requires a plug-in or a service account the AP clerk does not have.

The construction

The signature lives inside the invoice.

Filigree distributes an ECDSA P-256 signature across every character of the invoice via invisible Unicode carriers. To a human reader the signed and unsigned invoices look identical. To the verifier — a static web page that runs entirely in the browser — they are unmistakable. No certificate authority, no network call, no plug-in. All operations are local: the invoice never leaves the verifier's device.

Properties

What that buys you.

  1. Intrinsic
    The signature lives in the invoice text. Cannot be stripped without destroying the visible content.
  2. Verifier-friendly
    Paste invoice, paste public key, click. Under thirty seconds. No install, no certificate management.
  3. Binary
    Verification passes or fails. No partial results for an AP clerk to misinterpret under pressure.
  4. Transparent
    Signed invoices render identically in every email client, PDF reader, and ERP. Systems without Filigree are unaffected.
  5. Offline-verifiable
    The invoice and the vendor's public key are all the AP team needs. No CA, no OCSP, no blockchain.
  6. Standard cryptography
    ECDSA P-256 and SHA-256. The innovation is architectural — where the signature lives — not in the primitives.
Integration

Less than a sprint.

The reference implementation is a single static HTML file with no build step. Drop the engine into your invoice pipeline server-side; expose verification as a paste-and-click page for AP.
